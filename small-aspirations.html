Small Aspirations

Linh Dao

Project Description

Small Aspirations is based on critical play that critiques, subverts, and explores alternatives to the misunderstood immigration experience. I draw from both the traditional form of play (e.g., three object play) as well as video game play (e.g., puzzler) to inform the design of a web-based VR experience inspired by a fortune-telling custom in which a baby is given three objects to choose from at their first birthday, each of which symbolizes a path the child aspires to follow when they become adults. Here the player is a first-time immigrant to the U.S., choosing jobs such as restaurant and construction worker that are not glamorous but essential.

Artist Bio

Linh Dao is an interaction designer and educator whose creative and scholarly work explores the intersection between identity discourses and the development and implementation of creative and emerging technology. Her research interests include emigration, immigration, and migration, as well as minority equality and accessibility. Her work has been shown at SIGGRAPH SPARK, Ars Electronica, the Institute for Art and Innovation in Berlin, The American Institute of Graphic Arts Design Conference, and the Ammerman Center for Arts and Technology at Connecticut College. Her list of clients includes the American Cancer Society, Nonprofit New York, the National Institute for Early Education Research at Rutger University, the New York Historic District Council, and the GLBT+ Historical Society Museum in San Francisco. In addition to being awarded by UCDA, Indigo Design Award, Graphic Design USA, and Creative Quarterly Design, her work has also been published by the Design Research Society, International Association of Societies of Design Research, and The American Institute of Graphic Arts. She is currently an assistant professor at California Polytechnic State University.
