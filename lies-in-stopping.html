Lies in Stopping

Yuehan Hao

Project Description

Lies in Stopping explores how the ripples of our actions and relationships extend far beyond their origins. Yuehan Hao uses overlapping imagery and the metaphor of a clock to examine the judgmental consciousness of death and its impact on familial bonds. It reflects on how individual experiences of grief and loss influence and are influenced by broader societal connections. By transforming personal sorrow into public discourse, Lies in stopping highlights the responsibility and effects of our choices, illustrating the intricate web of human interdependence, enduring even after death. The piece invites contemplation on the nature of ends, the preservation of memory, and the therapeutic power of visual media in healing and reconstructing fractured family relationships.

Artist Bio

Yuehan Hao (b.2000) is an artist dedicated to visual creation. She graduated from Kede College of Capital Normal University with a photography major. Her creations mainly revolve around photography, combining documentary and conceptual images to explore the relationship between life, free consciousness, and bodily imagery through everyday observations and reflections. Her works have been exhibited in Shanghai, London, Greenville, Sao Paulo, and published in art magazines, spreading worldwide.
